# Dog Care Guide

## Daily Exercise Requirements
Dogs need regular physical activity to maintain health. Adult dogs typically require 30–60 minutes of exercise per day. High-energy breeds like Border Collies or Huskies may need 90+ minutes. Senior dogs (7+ years) benefit from shorter, more frequent walks of 15–20 minutes to protect joints. Puppies under 1 year need short sessions only — 5 minutes per month of age, twice daily — to protect developing joints.

## Feeding Guidelines
Feed adult dogs twice daily (morning and evening) at consistent times. Puppies under 6 months need 3–4 meals per day. Each feeding session typically takes 10–15 minutes. Always provide fresh water. Avoid feeding immediately before or after vigorous exercise to reduce bloat risk.

## Grooming
Brush short-haired dogs weekly (15 min). Long-haired breeds need daily brushing (20–30 min) to prevent matting. Nail trimming every 3–4 weeks (15 min). Ear cleaning monthly for floppy-eared breeds prevents infections. Bathing every 4–6 weeks or as needed.

## Medical Care
Dogs should receive heartworm preventative monthly (5 min to administer). Flea and tick prevention monthly. Annual wellness vet checkup. Dental brushing 2–3 times per week reduces dental disease risk by 70%. Semi-annual checkups for dogs over age 7.

## Enrichment and Mental Health
Mental stimulation is as important as physical exercise for dogs. Puzzle feeders, training sessions (10–15 min), and scent games prevent boredom and destructive behavior. Socialization with people and other dogs supports emotional wellbeing. Interactive play like fetch or tug counts as both exercise and enrichment.

## Special Needs: Senior Dogs
Dogs aged 7+ are considered seniors. They may need joint supplements, orthopedic bedding, and gentler exercise like swimming instead of running. Watch for signs of cognitive decline: confusion, changed sleep patterns, or house accidents. Semi-annual vet visits are recommended for early detection of kidney disease, heart disease, or cancer.

## Special Needs: Puppies
Puppies under 1 year require frequent short exercise sessions. Over-exercise damages developing growth plates. Socialization during the critical window of 8–16 weeks is essential for temperament. Puppies need more frequent meals (3–4 per day) and more frequent vet visits for vaccine series.
