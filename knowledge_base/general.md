# General Pet Care Principles

## Consistency and Routine
Consistent daily routines reduce stress for all pets. Feed, walk, and engage in enrichment at the same times each day. Predictable schedules help pets feel secure, make training easier, and allow owners to notice when a pet is off their normal pattern — an important early health signal.

## Preventive Health
Regular preventive care costs far less than treating illness. Schedule annual wellness exams, keep vaccinations current, and administer parasite prevention on a consistent schedule. Dental disease, obesity, and parasites are the most common and preventable pet health problems.

## Hydration
All pets need fresh water available at all times. Change water daily and clean the bowl regularly. A 10 kg dog needs approximately 500 ml of water per day; more in hot weather or after exercise. Cats often need encouragement to drink — wet food or water fountains help.

## Monitoring Health
Weigh pets monthly. Sudden weight loss or gain of more than 10% signals a health issue. Monitor energy levels, appetite, and bathroom habits daily — changes often indicate illness before other symptoms appear. Keep a simple log of any changes to share with your vet.

## Emergency Preparedness
Know your nearest 24-hour emergency vet and keep the number accessible. Maintain a basic pet first aid kit. Keep your pet's medical records (vaccination history, medications, allergies) in an accessible location. Know the signs of common emergencies: difficulty breathing, collapse, seizures, suspected poisoning, and bloat.

## Multiple Pets
When managing multiple pets, schedule care tasks to minimize interruptions and stress between animals. High-priority medical tasks (medications, special dietary needs) should always be scheduled first. Allow buffer time between pets for cleanup and transitions. Be aware of inter-pet dynamics — some pets need to be fed separately.

## Time Management for Pet Owners
Calculate total daily care time before adding a new pet. A single dog typically requires 1–2 hours of active care daily (feeding, walks, grooming, play). A cat requires 30–60 minutes. Multiple pets multiply this time requirement — plan your daily schedule accordingly and prioritize high-priority tasks when time is limited.
